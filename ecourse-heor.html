Background

Health Economics and Outcomes Research (HEOR) is an increasingly critical field that supports evidence-based healthcare decision-making by evaluating the economic, clinical, and humanistic value of healthcare interventions. With the growing complexity of healthcare systems, rising healthcare expenditures, and increased demand for cost-effective therapies, there is a pressing need for professionals trained in pharmacoeconomics, health technology assessment (HTA), real-world evidence (RWE), market access, and patient-centered outcomes research.

A substantial pool of healthcare professionals, pharmacists, clinicians, public health specialists, researchers, pharmaceutical professionals, policy experts, and academicians exists globally, yet formal HEOR training opportunities remain limited, particularly in accessible, flexible, and practice-oriented formats.

The eCourse in Health Economics and Outcomes Research is designed to bridge this educational gap by offering structured multidisciplinary training through an online learning platform. This course provides participants with foundational and applied knowledge in health economic evaluation, outcomes research, reimbursement strategy, and healthcare policy. Delivered over a duration of three months, the program combines self-paced learning, expert-led sessions, practical assignments, and industry-oriented projects to support learners in advancing their professional careers while balancing existing commitments.

Aim

The aim of this course is to provide participants with comprehensive interdisciplinary training in Health Economics and Outcomes Research to strengthen their ability to evaluate healthcare interventions, generate economic evidence, inform policy decisions, and contribute effectively to pharmaceutical, healthcare, and public health sectors.

Learning Objectives

By the end of the course, the participant should be able to:

  • Understand the fundamental concepts and scope of Health Economics and Outcomes Research
  • Apply pharmacoeconomic principles including cost-minimization, cost-effectiveness, cost-utility, and cost-benefit analyses
  • Interpret and conduct health economic evaluations for healthcare interventions
  • Analyze healthcare costs, reimbursement frameworks, and budget impact models
  • Understand and utilize real-world evidence and patient-reported outcomes data
  • Develop and critically evaluate Health Technology Assessment (HTA) frameworks
  • Understand market access strategies, pricing, and reimbursement pathways
  • Design HEOR studies and interpret outcomes data for policy and industry applications
  • Present HEOR findings in professional reports, value dossiers, and scientific manuscripts

Course Modules

The course follows a structured module-based learning approach over three months.

1
Foundations of HEOR & Research Methodology

Build a strong conceptual and methodological base in outcomes research.

  • Introduction to HEOR and its role in healthcare decision-making
  • Research methodology and study design frameworks used in HEOR
  • Overview of real-world evidence (RWE) and its applications
  • Fundamentals of biostatistics relevant to healthcare research
  • Key statistical concepts applied in outcomes and economic studies
2
Evidence Synthesis & Systematic Literature Review (SLR)

Develop expertise in structured evidence generation and critical appraisal.

  • Introduction to Systematic Literature Reviews (SLR) and their importance in HEOR
  • Step-by-step process for conducting high-quality systematic reviews
  • Designing search strategies and database navigation (PubMed training)
  • Critical appraisal of clinical evidence and risk-of-bias assessment
  • Application of SLR in clinical and outcomes research
3
Economic Evaluation, PROs & Health Technology Assessment (HTA)

Learn how evidence translates into value, access, and policy decisions.

  • Principles of pharmacoeconomics and economic evaluation methods (CEA, CUA, CBA)
  • Cost-effectiveness analysis and health economic modeling
  • Understanding and analyzing patient-reported outcomes (PROs)
  • Introduction to Health Technology Assessment (HTA) frameworks
  • Role of HTA agencies in pricing, reimbursement, and policy decisions
